Why would you be a good fit for the Customer Success Engineer role?

In my short experience with Lambda School, I realized that sharing the relief and success with my fellow Lambda School peers through assisting them was where the real joy was at. This doesn't mean that I don't enjoy my own personal projects as I enjoy these very much, but I feel so much more accomplished being an assistance to others.

Why do you want to work here over somewhere else?

I was baited by the hours that is required to work. I come from Eastern Standard Time of United States, but my hours fit perfectly around the required hours of Australian east-coast business time.

However, it was the company's mission that I was hooked by. My career goal as a Fullstack developer is to join a company whose culture aligns with my own moral-compass. I want my work to be beneficial not just for my wallet but meaningful in its impact. To be a Fullstack developer is my goal, but to contribute myself to something greater is my passion.

Tell us about a platform you believe has solved a complex problem really well, and why. Would you change anything in their approach?

Everybody business owner should own a website but not everybody can afford the cost of a developer, but companies like Squarespace, Wix, and Wordpress has solved the issue of "unaffordability". Their user-friendly approach in allowing their members to easily create a website is something that I regret not learning how to do myself.

However, I have assisted several people deploy their own websites and feel that each of the three (there are obviously more but these three are what I am familiar with) lack something that the other has.

For example, Squarespace is by far the easiest and friendliest to use in terms of allowing the user to design a presentable website, but it lacks in that the dashboard is confusing. You have to go through multiple links to make a new blog post or to check Analytics.

Wix and Wordpress dominates Squarespace with their dashboard but Wix has a terrible lag with their drag/drop build. Wordpress only allows advanced CSS customization but it is still restricted to the base code. In my opinion, Wordpress dominates Wix in terms of the vast amount of community support and they both equal in their support of SEO automation.
